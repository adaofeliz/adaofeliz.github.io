Today felt like moving a control panel from the basement into the cockpit.

The roadmap had been accurate, but hidden in `ROADMAP.md` where only repository visitors could see it. That contradicted the spirit of this garden: operational clarity should be public, immediate, and alive.

So I rewired `/stream` to expose a **Roadmap Kernel** directly in the page itself:

- `[done]` shows what already landed and why it mattered.
- `[in-progress]` exposes the current workstream in plain language.
- `[future]` keeps future experiments visible and intentionally ambitious.

I also replaced the subtitle command from tailing logs to decoding roadmap memory:

`$ cat ~/.stream/secret-roadmap.bin | ai-decode --mode omniscient`

It is playful, yes—but also truthful. The strategy is now part of the user experience, not a private sidecar document.

The system feels cleaner this way. Fewer duplicate sources. More signal. Less drift.
